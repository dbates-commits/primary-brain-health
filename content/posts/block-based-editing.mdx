Your daily habits have a profound impact on your brain health. While genetics play a role, research consistently shows that lifestyle factors are among the most powerful tools for reducing dementia risk. Here are five changes backed by strong evidence.

## 1. Regular Aerobic Exercise

Studies show that 150 minutes of moderate aerobic exercise per week can significantly reduce the risk of cognitive decline. Exercise increases blood flow to the brain, promotes neuroplasticity, and supports the growth of new neurons in the hippocampus — the brain's memory center.

## 2. Quality Sleep

Poor sleep is increasingly linked to the accumulation of beta-amyloid plaques, a hallmark of Alzheimer's disease. Aim for 7-8 hours of consistent, quality sleep each night. If you suspect a sleep disorder, getting evaluated is one of the highest-impact steps you can take.

## 3. Cognitive Engagement

Challenging your brain with new learning, complex problem-solving, or skill acquisition helps build cognitive reserve. This doesn't mean brain games — it means activities that genuinely push you to think in new ways.

## 4. Social Connection

Loneliness and social isolation are recognized risk factors for dementia. Maintaining strong social connections and engaging in regular, meaningful social activities supports both emotional and cognitive health.

## 5. Heart-Healthy Diet

What's good for your heart is good for your brain. A Mediterranean-style diet rich in vegetables, whole grains, fish, and healthy fats has been associated with lower rates of cognitive decline in multiple large-scale studies.
