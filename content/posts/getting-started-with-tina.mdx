Dementia doesn't start when symptoms appear. By the time most people notice cognitive changes, the underlying processes have been developing for years — sometimes decades. That's why early brain health screening is so critical.

## The Case for Early Intervention

Research published in The Lancet Commission on Dementia Prevention identified up to 12 modifiable risk factors that account for roughly 40% of dementia cases worldwide. Many of these factors — including hypertension, hearing loss, physical inactivity, and social isolation — can be addressed years before cognitive symptoms emerge.

## What a Brain Health Screening Involves

A comprehensive brain health screening goes beyond a simple memory test. It typically includes a validated cognitive assessment, a review of your medical history and family risk factors, lifestyle evaluation, and in some cases, biomarker testing. The goal is to establish a baseline and identify areas for intervention.

## Taking the First Step

If you have a family history of dementia, are noticing subtle cognitive changes, or simply want to be proactive about your brain health, a screening is a smart first step. The earlier you start, the more options you have.
