Design a single, high-converting landing page for Primary Brain Health (PBH), a virtual-first brain health enablement and dementia prevention clinic launching a new digital presence.

The target audience is health-conscious adults aged 40 and older who value data-driven, evidence-based care. There are three key audience segments: individuals with a family history of dementia seeking proactive prevention, people noticing early cognitive changes in memory, focus, or word-finding, and executives and professionals who want to optimize their long-term cognitive performance. The design must also communicate expertise to potential business partners.

The primary goal is to establish PBH as a credible, clinically grounded brand and drive visitors toward one action: booking an initial brain health consultation via a custom intake form. The design should feel clinically grounded yet warm and approachable, trustworthy without being cold or sterile. Think modern healthcare meets trusted advisor.

The page is a single scrolling landing page with seven sections in this order:

Section 1 is the Hero. It features a bold value proposition with the primary call-to-action button labeled "Book a Consultation" and supporting imagery. Consider a split layout with the headline and CTA on one side and a warm, professional image on the other.

Section 2 is About / What We Do. This provides a clear explanation of PBH's virtual-first brain health model and clinical approach. Two short paragraphs of body text. Clean, readable typography with generous whitespace.

Section 3 is Who It's For. Three cards with icons and short descriptions targeting the three audience segments: family history of dementia, noticing early cognitive changes, and optimizing long-term brain function.

Section 4 is How It Works. A four-step overview of the patient journey: Book Your Consultation, Complete Your Brain Health Assessment, Receive Your Personalized Care Plan, and Begin Your Brain Health Journey. Each step has an icon and a short description. This should feel simple and reassuring to reduce anxiety about the process.

Section 5 is Credibility and Trust. Three trust signals with icons and short descriptions: Board-Certified Neurologists, Evidence-Based Protocols, and HIPAA-Compliant Virtual Care.

Section 6 is the Custom Intake Form. A multi-field form collecting full name, email address, phone number, birth year, sex, years of education, whether the person is contacting on behalf of themselves or someone else, and an optional message field. The submit button is labeled "Book a Consultation." The form should feel inviting and straightforward, not bureaucratic.

Section 7 is the Footer. It contains anchor links back to page sections, a contact email address, legal links for privacy policy and terms of service, and social media icons.

There is a sticky header at the top of the page with the logo on the left, section anchor navigation links in the center reading About, Who It's For, How It Works, and Why Us, and a "Book a Consultation" call-to-action button on the right. The header has a solid white background. The currently visible section should be highlighted in the navigation as an active state.

The sections should alternate between white and off-white backgrounds for visual separation. Use generous padding between sections. The page should breathe.

The visual direction should be professional and modern, resonating with adults over 40. Use a calming, trustworthy color palette such as deep blues, teals, or sage greens paired with warm neutrals. The accent color for call-to-action buttons should stand out clearly. Typography should be a clean modern sans-serif with generous sizing for headlines and highly readable body text for the older demographic. Use subtle borders or soft shadows on cards with rounded corners and light background fills.

For imagery, use warm and authentic photography of diverse adults aged 40 to 70. Avoid generic stock photo smiles. Consider abstract neuroscience-inspired patterns or subtle neural motifs as texture elements but not literal brain illustrations. Do not use stethoscope or lab coat imagery.

The overall mood should be reassuring, intelligent, and forward-looking. The visitor should feel they are in good hands and taking a smart, proactive step. Avoid overly clinical or hospital-like aesthetics, dense walls of text, and aggressive urgency tactics like countdown timers or fear-based messaging.

The design must be fully responsive across desktop, tablet, and mobile breakpoints with a mobile-first approach. It must meet WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility standards. The content is managed through a CMS so the design should accommodate variable text lengths for headlines, descriptions, and body copy. Brand assets including logo files, colors, and typefaces will be integrated once delivered by the client. The design concepts should establish a visual direction that can absorb these assets.

The technical stack is Next.js with TypeScript, Tailwind CSS for styling, Tina CMS for content editing, and Vercel for hosting. The foundation should be architected to support future additions including a patient portal, online scheduling, a blog, HIPAA compliance, and an AI chatbot without significant rework.

This is not a multi-page website. It is a single landing page only. There is no blog, patient portal, scheduling integration, or e-commerce in this version. No custom illustrations are needed. Use generative AI imagery or stock photography. No advanced animations. Focus on clean, performant interactions.
